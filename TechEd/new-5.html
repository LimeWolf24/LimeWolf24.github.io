Sanders, Saws, and Drills~

Safe use of Sanders, Saws, and Drills:

Sanders~


Belt Sander- Belt sanders can have different speeds. Belt sanders are usually used for bigger projects' edges and sides. make sure to be careful with your fingers.



Electric Hand Sanders- These are good for finish sanding, before staining or painting wood.



Sandpaper and Sanding Block- Sandpaper is used for small touch ups and smoothing. Sanding Blocks are blocks of wood with sides of sandpaper, some people use scrap peices of wood and wrap sandpaper around it, which works just as well. When using sandpaper, the higher the number, the less friction, the lower the number the more friction and grit. Meaning start with the lower numbered sandpapers and move up as you finish the project.


Saws~


Bandsaw- Bandsaws are good for "free-cutting", suck as cutting curves, but make sure not to turn the blade too much or it might snap. Bandsaws are also good for straight cuts. Bandsaws are the basic starter saw.



Miter Saw- Miter saws are good for strait cuts and angled cuts. Miter saws are definitely an easy to learn saw, they are pretty straightforward.



Scroll Saw- The scroll saw moves in an up and down motion and the speed is changeable, two hands are recommended on the stock (wood peice) at all times while cutting with any saw- as well as keep your fingers from the blade. A scroll saw is usually used for curves and cutting in holes in the stock for the project.

Drills~


Drill Press- Drill Press is used for drilling holes in the stock for clocks, wall plaques,



Hand Drill- Hand drills are good for smaller holes, and putting in screws. Be careful where you put your finger.
